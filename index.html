MALAYSIA

History

Malaysia has a rich history that dates back to the 2nd century AD. The country has been influenced by many different cultures over the years, including Indian, Chinese, and European cultures. Here are some key events in Malaysia's history:

• 2nd century AD: The Malay kingdom of Langkasuka is established on the Malay Peninsula.

• 1400s: The Malacca Sultanate is established.

• 1800s: The British colonize Malaysia.

• 1948: The Federation of Malaya is established.

• 1963: Malaysia is formed after the merger of the Federation of Malaya, Singapore, Sabah, and Sarawak.

Today, Malaysia is a diverse and multicultural country with a vibrant economy and a rich cultural heritage.

Travelling Spots

The Petronas Twin Towers and Batu Caves are two famous travelling spots in Malaysia.

Food

Background

Malaysian food is a delicious and diverse cuisine that has been shaped by its multi-ethnic population, which includes Malay, Chinese, Indian, and indigenous influences.

Malaysian cuisine is a delectable blend of Malay, Chinese, Indian, and indigenous influences, reflecting the country's multi-ethnic population.

Its bold flavors, vibrant colors, and diverse textures are a reflection of its tropical climate and rich cultural heritage.

Each region of Malaysia has its own distinct culinary specialties, ingredients, and cooking techniques, offering a tantalizing range of dishes from street food stalls to high-end restaurants.

Roti Canai

(1) Roti Canai is a popular Malaysian flatbread that originated from India.

(2) The dough is stretched, flipped, and folded several times to create thin layers of dough.

(3) It is cooked on a flat griddle until crispy and golden brown.

(4) Roti canai is often served with a variety of flavorful curries or sweet condensed milk/sugar.

(5) It is a staple breakfast or snack food in Malaysia and can be found at local street stalls, Indian Muslim restaurants, and mamak eateries.



Nasi Lemak

(1) Nasi Lemak is Malaysia's national dish, consisting of fragrant rice cooked in coconut milk and pandan leaves.

(2) The rice has a sweet aroma and greenish color from pandan leaves and a creamy texture from coconut milk.

(3) It is served with various side dishes, such as spicy sambal chili sauce, crispy anchovies, cucumber slices, peanuts, and a hard-boiled or fried egg.

Culture

Malaysia is a diverse and multicultural country with a rich heritage.

Its culture consists of a fusion between Malay, Chinese, Indian, and indigenous traditions, reflected in its architecture, art, music, festivals, and cuisine.

Family, community, and traditional values of respect, hospitality, and harmony are highly valued in Malaysian culture.

The blend of ancient customs and modern influences make Malaysian culture a fascinating and vibrant aspect of the country's identity.
